Other Routes
and Highways

There are some other routes and highways at the final administrative level of the American Highway system including county highways, forest highways, and bicycle routes. The table down below takes you on a road trip down all the different types of other routes and highways we have in America besides Interstate highways, U.S. highways, and state routes.

Other Routes and Highways
Sign Name Amount Year of Formation Information
County Highway About 10,000 1806 Routes maintained by different counties of America. Vary from well-traveled multilane highways to dirt roads into remote parts of the county. Signs are designed with a blue pentagon with a yellow border and the route number and designated county within them or a white square with a black background and the route number and designated county within them.
U.S. Bicycle Route 55 1978 Routes that consist of interstate long-distance cycling routes that use multiple types of bicycling infrastructure, including off-road paths, bicycle lanes, and low-traffic roads.
Branson Red Route 1 1956 A.K.A. Shepard of the Hills Expressway, A northern extension of Highway 76 that serves as an alternative route and runs parallel to Highway 76. Connects to Highway 65 at its exit.
Charlotte Route 4 1 1981 Partial ring road that serves Charlotte, North Carolina and runs a half loop around I-85. Sign designed with a green pentagon and Charlotte's crown logo.
Forest Highway 13 1921 Highways connecting national forests to existing state highway systems, providing access to recreational and logging areas. Defined as "a forest under the jurisdiction of, and maintained by, a public authority and open to public travel." Signs are designed as brown isosceles trapezoids with white borders and white horizontal lines separating the route numbers from the "National Forest" writitngs below them.
Indian Route 27 1926 Routes found on some Indian reservations. Signs are designed with white shields with varying designs depending on the state and/or reservation, and a black background. Maintained by the Bureau of Indian Affairs (BIA), and/or a given tribal nation.
Township Routes 1,308 1820 Routes maintained by townships in Ohio.
Allegheny County Belt System Routes 6 1951 Routes serving the Allegheny County Belt System in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania. Consists of 6 beltways: Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, and Purple.
Rochester Inner Loop 1 1965 A C-shaped freeway that serves Rochester, New York. Sign designed as a white square with an orange background, and an orange trapezoid containing "Inner Loop" within it.
